    Based on the above "strengths and weaknesses" lists I have a better idea of how I will manage myself personally, and based off of what may be a universal student learning agreement basic I will endeavour to:

    • Actively participate in the lesson and be fully engaged at all times;
    • Actively seek feedback on how to improve the quality of my work;
    • Be resilience and persevere with all challenges, even when they are difficult; Try to sort out problems myself but also seek guidance from peers and facilitators;
    • Manage own time and be self-disciplined;
    • Uses initiative and don't always need to be told what to do;
    • Goes above and beyond what the facilitator asks of them.

    Personally I will endeavour to improve on what I know are my weaknesses, especially if I want to be successful not only in the Foundations/Bootcamp program, but also in the professional/working environment.

    - Self-awareness - realise when I'm struggling early on. Stay aware of how much time is left to complete by deadline. Estimate how much time you need to complete a exercise. If struggling follow methods of problem solving/identify what exactly it is you are struggling with. If you need more time talk to Kelly ASAP. Don't wait.

    - Planning/Organising - Use your planner to note due dates and commitments. Keep mindful of what commitments require.

    - Timeliness - instead of snoozing your early alarm - on the first ring open up netflix and watch a cartoon. This will wake you up slowly but effectively.
    Get a bicycle when you get enough money.

    - Learn how to Negotiate. Learn why you have low ambition or an avoidance of challenges. Ask what makes someone good at self-management. Know how to best deal with someone who is draining your assertiveness. Ask around of industry vets how to manage mental health when it starts to impede and how to plan for bad days - like - best management practice.

    - If unsure about taking the initiative ask questions - clarify a little, and then leap in. I should be able to learn on the fly.

    - Communication and Check-in. Keep up a weekly dialogue with your facilitator through slack (or onsite) to ensure that yourself and your facilitator know where you are up to at anytime. Message Kelly every Friday on Slack after checking checklist.

    - Lists - create a checklist of things I need to do throughout Foundations and Bootcamp to have something to checkoff in order to ensure consistency and completion. Lists are an essential tool to aid in the case of inattention and forgetfulness.

    I will try to recognise early on when I may need to ask for help, though I will appreciate it if the facilitator can see me struggling, to please let me know or to ask me if I need any help.

    From the facilitators, I would appreciate from them:

    • To have an idea of where I should be (if obviously I am not D: )/ assignments I should be working on at any time.
    • To push me when needed
    • Not allow themselves to be bogged down with a slack student?
    • Know enough about individuals and their contribution to team dynamics to make it effective
    • Not allow distractions
    • Motivate us. Help us to envision the bigger picture so we are not lost in the current minutiae of what we are learning.
    • Clear instructions that don't jump from one place to another out of context
    • Point out the obvious when needed
    • Call out things that may not be right
    • Don't let us stumble on through in our ignorance :(
    • Allow us to unwind as necessary - ie. exercise or rest if stressed/struggling

    Using this very raw, first attempt at writing my own Learning Agreement I will endeavour to do my best and if changes are needed then be flexible enough to make changes as needed.
