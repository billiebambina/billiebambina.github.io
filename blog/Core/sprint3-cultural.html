    What is Emotional Intelligence?

    EI or EQ (emotional quotient) is apparently the ability to read a person and (one would hope) read a room. This allows a person to behave in a complex yet seemingly simple way when interacting with anything. Be it Human, cute bunny, or a ball with a face drawn on it.

    How is it different to IQ?

    IQ (intelligence quotient) is only how well a person does on a particular quiz. Unfortunately the term doesn't even encompass the wide range of intelligences out there - such as being illiterate but at the same time an amazing business person, a savant who couldn't read any of this opinion piece but inspires movies about code breaking government secrets.

    But in general IQ is just a way to say that you are very smart.

    Why is it important?

    It is important to be able to balanced in terms of your emotions and intelligence rationale.

    If you were to be 'over-emotional' you may be more inclined to be biased towards the feelings of others and yourself instead of the progression and functioning of a common objective.

    The reverse would be to be so rational that the objectives are to be met regardless of how it may hurt the others that are trying to achieve the same objective. (I fatally think of Capitalism...)

    So in my understanding of EQ, it is a way to function as someone who is knowledgeable in a particular field, but also possesses a character that is agreeable/personable when dealing with peers, clients and in general.

    Though, when talking about 'Emotions' per se, there are two ways that emotion is responded to: Sympathetic, and Empathetic.

    Both have their pros and cons and to be thoroughly biased I hate sympathetic people. Sympathetic people may be able to see that someone is having a hard time but they'd be the type to sit there on the sideline making comments about how glad they aren't that person. AN EMPATHETIC PERSON WILL GO OVER TO THAT PERSON AND SEE HOW THEY CAN HELP. Accessibility is definitely the domain of the empathetic problem solver.
